What is contra dance?

In a contra dance, guided by a caller, each couple dances figures with a series of other couples. It’s great fun. The two roles are called “larks” and “robins” and they are each danced by whoever wants to, no matter their genders and it is common to see same-gendered couples.

{% elsif page.lang == "fr" %}

Qu’est ce qu’une contredanse de la Nouvelle-Angleterre?

Dans une contredanse, un couple guidé par un calleur danse une séquence de figures avec d’autres couples. Les rôles s’appellent « Larks » et « Robins ». Le choix d’un rôle est personnel et il est fréquent de voir des couples formés de personnes du même sexe.

How much dancing experience do I need?

None! Contra dance is very beginner-friendly. There is no fancy footwork to learn. All dances are taught. If this this your first time at a contra dance, we recommend that you come to the introdcutory lesson to learn the basics.

Do I need to have a partner?

You can come alone, bring a partner or a friend, or come with a group! You can dance with anyone who is there and you will change partners for each dance. You will get to dance with many different people of all ages, genders, and experience levels throughout the evening!

What is the music like?

We always dance to live music played by local musicians or travelling bands. You will here mostly traditional Québécois, Irish, or New England music played on fiddles, acoustic guitars, and the like. But every band is different!
